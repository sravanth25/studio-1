15 Critical Red Flags in Property Documents
1. Broken Ownership Chain

One of the biggest risks in any property transaction is an incomplete or broken ownership chain.

What Is Ownership Chain Verification?

Ownership chain verification tracks the transfer history of a property from one owner to another over a defined period, usually 30–40 years.

Red Flags to Watch
Missing transfer documents
Sudden ownership jumps
Unregistered transfers
Inheritance gaps
Missing partition deeds
Why It Matters

A broken chain creates uncertainty in title ownership and may invalidate a future transaction.

Verification Tip

Always cross-check:

Sale deeds
Gift deeds
Partition deeds
Release deeds
Legal heir records
2. Existing Mortgage or Charge on Property

A property may already be mortgaged to a bank or financial institution.

Key Risk

Purchasing or financing a mortgaged property without disclosure can trigger major legal disputes.

Documents to Check
Encumbrance Certificate (EC)
CERSAI records
Bank NOCs
Mortgage release deeds
Common Red Flags
Active loan entries
Equitable mortgage references
Unreleased charges
Missing loan closure documentation
Importance of Mortgage Verification

A strong mortgage verification process helps prevent financial fraud and lending risks.

Best Practice
